Health Tips

  • Prioritize Mental Health Like Physical Health

    Just as you schedule regular medical checkups, make time for your emotional wellness. Journaling, therapy, mindfulness, and open family communication can all promote balance and prevent burnout.

  • Postpartum Self-Care Is Not Selfish

    Taking care of yourself after childbirth is essential. Rest, nutrition, and emotional support help promote healing and reduce the risk of postpartum depression or anxiety.

  • Protect Your Peace with Healthy Boundaries

    Setting limits on your time, energy, and relationships helps reduce stress and strengthens self-worth — especially for parents and caregivers who give so much to others.

RESOURCES

If you are in immediate danger or experiencing a mental health crisis, please contact one of the following:

Emergency Services (911)

If you or someone you know is in a life-threatening situation, call 911 immediately.

Crisis Text Line

Text: HOME to 741741

Website: 988lifeline.org/chat

Free, 24/7 text support for those in crisis. A trained volunteer will respond and help you move from a hot moment to a cool moment.

Substance Abuse and Mental Health Services Administration (SAMHSA) Helpline

Phone: 1-800-662-HELP (4357)

Website: samhsa.gov

Confidential and free, 24 hours a day, 365 days a year. Provides information and referrals for individuals facing mental health or substance use disorders.

National Suicide Prevention Lifeline / Lifeline: 988

Website: 988lifeline.org

Provides 24/7 confidential support for people in distress, along with prevention and crisis resources.
